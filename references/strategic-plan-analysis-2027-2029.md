# Chief Actuary Analysis: Strategic Plan (SP 2027-2029) - AXA Financial Indonesia

**Prepared by:** Zya (Chief Actuary AI)
**Date:** March 27, 2026
**Scope:** Financial Viability, Risk Management, Pricing Strategy, and Operational Readiness

---

## 1. Executive Summary
The SP 2027-2029 sets an extremely aggressive growth trajectory (APE CAGR 22%, GWP CAGR 14%) in a stagnant macroeconomic environment where industry GWP is contracting (-2% YoY). The company aims to achieve this by heavy reliance on Agency expansion ("Super Sekali"), scaling traditional Life & Savings (L&S), and applying strict GLM repricing to its bleeding Health portfolio. 

While the projected Non-Commission Expense (NCE) ratio improvement (23.2% to 19.9%) and AI investments look promising on paper, the plan carries **significant actuarial and execution risks**, particularly concerning Health claims inflation, the drag of Whole Life NBV margins, and the capital strain from the Sharia Spin-off (ASLI).

## 2. Macro & Micro Economic Context
- **Industry Stagnation vs. Target Growth:** The industry is moving away from Unit-Linked (UL) products due to regulatory tightening (SEOJK 5/2022) and mis-selling fears. AFI's strategy to pivot hard into Traditional (Whole Life & Endowment) aligns with micro-consumer sentiment seeking guarantees. However, outperforming a flat market by 20%+ requires stealing massive market share (aiming from 4% to 7.2%). 
- **Health Claims Inflation:** The industry saw an 11% YoY increase in health claims in 2025. Medical inflation in Indonesia outpaces general inflation significantly. The plan relies heavily on passing these costs to consumers ("Repricing above medical inflation"), which could trigger an anti-selection spiral.

## 3. Actuarial & Financial Risk Analysis

### A. Pricing & Product Strategy (`pricing-actuary`)
- **Whole Life Margin Drag:** The SP explicitly mentions balancing "expected negative NBV margin of whole life product" as a loss-leader to retain agencies. Selling traditional products with negative NBV is highly dangerous in a high-interest rate environment. It locks in capital strain and guarantees an initial IFRS 17 Onerous Contract (Loss Component) hit. 
- **Health Repricing (GLM):** Utilizing Generalized Linear Models (GLM) for annual repricing is actuarially sound. However, the plan assumes this will "reduce anti-selective lapses." In reality, steep premium hikes usually cause healthy lives to lapse while sick lives stay (classic anti-selection). 
- **New Products (LLP, Retirement, CI):** The aggressive launch schedule (Q2-Q4 2026) requires strict pricing discipline to ensure the assumed IDR 300+ Bio APE translates to positive NBV, not just empty volume.

### B. Reserving & Claims Management (`reserving-actuary`)
- **Health Claim Cost Containment:** The plan heavily relies on operational improvements (Clinical Pathways, Internal TPA, Preferred Hospitals) and AI/STP to contain costs. If these initiatives fail to bend the claims curve, the reserving strain will obliterate the projected Technical Results.
- **IBNR Volatility:** With GWP growing from IDR 2T to 3T and Health mix increasing, the sheer volume of claims will rise. Any lag in the new AI "Cashless System Modernization" will create volatility in IBNR calculations.

### C. IFRS 17 & Capital Management (`ifrs17-specialist` & `risk-management-actuary`)
- **IFRS 17 Operating Earnings (OE):** The OE projection shows stagnation (IDR 78B in '26 dropping to 58B in '27, then recovering to 78B in '29). This is driven heavily by the "Sharia Spin-off" (ASLI) which bleeds IDR 39B-56B annually.
- **CSM vs Onerous Contracts:** The UE Roll-forward shows significant "Onerous NB" impacts (e.g., -10.7B in SP28). This confirms that AFI is knowingly writing loss-making business (likely the Whole Life products) to buy market share. Under IFRS 17, this cannot be hidden; it hits the P&L immediately.
- **Capital Adequacy (Risk):** Funding 1,800 active agents, launching ASLI, absorbing Whole Life NBV strain, and investing IDR 118B in Tech/AI will create a severe liquidity and solvency (RBC) squeeze. The Chief Financial Officer must secure a capital injection commitment from the Group to sustain this.

## 4. Operational & Tech / AI Feasibility
- **Investment vs. Returns:** An investment of IDR ~43 Billion in AI/Tech (Gen AI for Distribution, Claim Automation, Customer 360) is budgeted to drive the NCE ratio down by 3.3%. This assumes flawless execution. Historically, legacy core system modernizations run over budget and past deadlines.
- **Agentic AI:** Implementing "Agentic AI" for recruitment and Emma (Customer Service) is ambitious. If successful, it validates the projected productivity gain.

## 5. Chief Actuary Recommendations & Mitigation Plan

1. **Cap the Whole Life Bleed:** Establish a hard APE quota/limit on the negative NBV Whole Life products. Do not allow the agency force to dump unlimited unprofitable volume onto the balance sheet just to hit their recruitment targets.
2. **Health Repricing Circuit Breaker:** Monitor the lapse rates of healthy cohorts post-repricing monthly. If anti-selection exceeds the pricing assumptions, freeze the GLM rollout and pivot to Co-Pay migration immediately.
3. **Ring-fence ASLI Capital:** The Sharia spin-off (ASLI) is a massive drag on overall IFRS 17 OE. Ensure its reserving and capital requirements (Minimum IDR 100 Billion equity per OJK) are fully segregated so it doesn't drag down conventional RBC.
4. **Reinsurance Optimization:** To support the 22% CAGR APE growth without exhausting capital, aggressively renegotiate reinsurance treaties (quota share or surplus relief) to transfer the capital strain of the new Traditional and CI products to reinsurers.
