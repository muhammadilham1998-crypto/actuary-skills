# Regulatory Framework - Indonesia (OJK & PSAK)

This document contains extracted baseline rules and parameters for Actuarial practice in Indonesia. When calculating reserves, capital, or pricing products for the Indonesian market, always refer to these parameters.

> **Note:** For full legal clauses, refer to the original PDFs in your local workspace or vector database.

## 1. Risk-Based Capital (RBC) - OJK
- **Minimum Target RBC:** 120%
- **Definition:** Available Capital divided by Required Capital (Batas Tingkat Solvabilitas Minimum / BTSM).
- **Intervention Triggers:** If RBC falls below 120%, the company must submit a financial restructuring plan to OJK.

## 2. Minimum Capital Requirements (Ekuitas Minimum)
- **Life Insurance (Asuransi Jiwa):** Rp 100 Billion
- **General Insurance (Asuransi Umum):** Rp 100 Billion
- **Reinsurance (Reasuransi):** Rp 200 Billion

## 3. Financial Reporting (PSAK 74 / IFRS 17)
- **Effective Date:** January 1, 2025 (mandated by Ikatan Akuntan Indonesia / IAI).
- **Key Impacts:** 
  - Revenue recognition must shift from premium received to insurance services provided.
  - Onerous contracts must be recognized immediately in P&L.
  - Requires calculation of Risk Adjustment (RA) and Contractual Service Margin (CSM).

## 4. Product Pricing (PAYDI / Unit Link) - SEOJK 5/2022
- **Premium Allocation:** Front-end loads and acquisition costs are strictly capped to ensure a minimum percentage of the premium is invested in the early policy years.
- **Capital Guarantee:** Unit-linked products generally do not guarantee principal; the investment risk is fully borne by the policyholder.
- **Illustration Rates:** Maximum illustration rates (e.g., Low, Medium, High) must comply with OJK caps based on historical benchmark returns.

## 5. Reserving Requirements
- **Valuation Rate:** Discount rates for long-term reserves must be tied to the Indonesian Government Bond (SBN - Surat Berharga Negara) yield curve, with appropriate illiquidity premiums.
- **Mortality/Morbidity Tables:** TMI (Tabel Mortalitas Indonesia) must be used as the baseline for life products, with specific adjustments based on company experience.
